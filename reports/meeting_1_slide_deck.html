Project Overview & Objectives

  • Predict heart-related health risks from European survey data (~40k records).
  • Deliver individual-level explanations with LIME and SHAP.
  • Surface results through a Gradio web interface and containerise with Docker.
  • Week 1–2 focus: data understanding, preprocessing pipeline, baseline models.
Slide 2

Dataset & EDA Highlights

  • Size: 42,377 records · 52 engineered features (23 numeric / 29 categorical).
  • Missingness: 0.25% overall (raw); Target balance: hltprhc = 1 → 11.32%.
  • Generated feature dictionary and mapping files for all encoded columns.
  • EDA outputs: missing-value summary, correlation heatmaps, VIF, IQR outliers (stored in results/metrics & results/plots).
Slide 3

Baseline Modeling Results

  • Models: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, 2-layer PyTorch NN.
  • Best recall: Logistic Regression (Accuracy ≈0.755 · Recall ≈0.72 · F1 ≈0.40).
  • Tree/NN models reach Accuracy ≈0.89 but Recall drops to 0.15–0.22 (majority-class bias).
  • Full metrics, confusion matrices, ROC & PR curves saved in results/metrics, results/confusion_matrices, results/plots.
Slide 4

Error Analysis & Insights

  • Logistic Regression produces most false positives, even with high predicted probabilities.
  • Tree/NN models are conservative—few positives detected → target for recall improvements.
  • Classification reports expose class imbalance (macro-F1 < 0.45 across models).
  • Misclassified samples exported (results/metrics/misclassified_samples.csv) for deeper Week 3–4 analysis.

Next Steps (Weeks 3–4)
